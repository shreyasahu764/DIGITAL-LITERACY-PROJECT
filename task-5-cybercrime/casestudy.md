## Case Study: UPI / Online Transaction Fraud

UPI/online transaction fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers manipulate users into transferring money or revealing sensitive financial information through digital payment platforms. These frauds exploit a lack of awareness and trust in online systems.

Typically, the fraud occurs in a step-by-step manner. First, the scammer contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or social media, pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. Next, they create urgency by mentioning account issues, refunds, or rewards. Then, they send a payment request or a malicious link and instruct the victim to approve it or enter an OTP. The victim, believing the request is genuine, unknowingly authorizes the transaction. Finally, the money is transferred instantly to the fraudster’s account.

Students, elderly people, and new digital payment users are the most common targets due to limited awareness. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and reduced trust in online systems, with recovery often being difficult.

---

## Fictional Scenario

Riya, a college student, received a call from someone claiming to be a customer care executive from her UPI app. The caller informed her that she was eligible for a ₹2,000 cashback reward. To claim it, she was asked to approve a request sent to her app. Without verifying, Riya followed the instructions. Instead of receiving money, ₹2,000 was deducted from her account.

Panicked, she tried contacting the number again, but it was unreachable. She later realized she had been scammed. This incident taught her the importance of verifying requests and never approving unknown transactions.
